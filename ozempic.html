Full Description: What Is Ozempic?

Ozempic® (semaglutide) is a GLP-1 receptor agonist originally approved for the treatment of Type 2 diabetes. It mimics the natural GLP-1 hormone, which plays a key role in glucose regulation, appetite control, and insulin sensitivity.

How Ozempic Works

  • Stimulates glucose-dependent insulin release
  • Reduces glucagon secretion
  • Slows gastric emptying
  • Reduces appetite and food cravings
  • Improves insulin sensitivity

Clinical Benefits

  • HbA1c reduction of 1.0% – 1.8%
  • Average weight loss of 10–20%
  • Reduced need for multiple daily injections
  • Lower risk of hypoglycemia compared to insulin

Who May Benefit

  • Adults with Type 2 diabetes
  • Patients struggling with weight gain on insulin
  • Individuals with cardiovascular risk factors
  • Patients seeking simplified treatment regimens

Evidence From Major Research Studies

Ozempic and semaglutide have been evaluated in multiple large-scale randomized clinical trial programs, including SUSTAIN, STEP, and SELECT. These trials provide high-quality evidence on glycemic control, weight loss, and cardiovascular protection.

SUSTAIN Trials (Type 2 Diabetes)

  • HbA1c reduction: 1.0% – 1.8%
  • Weight loss: 5–10 kg in many patients
  • Superior to several older diabetes medications

STEP Trials (Obesity)

  • Average weight loss: 14.9% – 17.4%
  • Long-term sustainability up to 2+ years
  • Significant improvement in metabolic markers

SELECT Trial (Cardiovascular Outcomes)

  • 20% reduction in major cardiovascular events
  • Lower heart attack and stroke rates
  • Benefits seen even without diabetes

Conclusion
